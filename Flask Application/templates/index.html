Men's ODI Cricket World Cup

The 2023 ICC Men's Cricket World Cup the 13th edition of the ICC Men's Cricket World Cup, a quadrennial One Day International (ODI) cricket tournament organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC). It was hosted from 5 October to 19 November 2023 across ten venues in India. This was the fourth World Cup held in India, but the first where India was the sole host.

The tournament was contested by ten national teams, maintaining the same format used in 2019. After six weeks of round-robin matches, India, South Africa, Australia, and New Zealand finished as the top four and qualified for the knockout stage. In the knockout stage, India and Australia beat New Zealand and South Africa , respectively, to advance to the finalon 19 November at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad. Australia won the final by six wickets, winning their sixth Cricket World Cup title.

Virat Kohli was named the player of the tournament and also scored the most runs, while Mohammed Shami was the leading wicket-taker. A total of 1,250,307 spectators attended the matches, the highest number in any Cricket World Cup to date.[1] The tournament final set viewership records in India, drawing 518 million viewers, with a peak of 57 million streaming viewers

Format

This was the first ICC World Cup in which penalties for slow over-rates were given to bowling sides if they did not complete their 50 overs in the stipulated time. On-field umpires could penalise the bowling team by not allowing more than four fielders outside the 30-yard circle.

Prize Money

The ICC allocated a pool of US$10 million in prize money for the tournament, with payouts remaining the same as the 2019 and 2015 tournaments. Australia, the winning team, received US$4,000,000, the runner-up $2,000,000 and the losing semi-finalists $1,600,000. Teams that did not progress past the league stage received $100,000 and the winner of each league stage match received $40,000.

Extras Contribution of Teams

In the 2023 Cricket World Cup, extras played a significant role in boosting team totals, often providing crucial additional runs in tight matches. Extras include wides, no-balls, byes, and leg-byes, which contribute to a team's score without requiring batters to play a shot.

Ultimately, managing extras was a key factor in a team's overall discipline and success in the 2023 World Cup, with teams maintaining tighter control over their bowling generally performing better in the tournament.

Teams Win While Batting First

In the 2023 Cricket World Cup, teams Batting first had a significant advantage in many matches, often securing wins by posting high totals and putting pressure on the chasing sides. The tournament saw several instances where teams successfully defended their scores, thanks to favorable pitch conditions, scoreboard pressure, and strong bowling performances.

Overall, the 2023 World Cup reinforced the effectiveness of batting first, especially when teams capitalized on good batting conditions and executed their bowling plans efficiently.

Teams Win While Fielding First

In the 2023 Cricket World Cup, several teams found success while Fielding first, leveraging early bowling pressure, favorable pitch conditions, and effective run chases. Teams like Australia, India, and Pakistan performed well when bowling first, often restricting opponents to manageable totals and chasing with confidence.

As a result, while setting a target remained a dominant strategy, chasing teams also had their share of success in the 2023 World Cup, proving the importance of adaptability in different match situations.
